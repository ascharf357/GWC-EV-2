What We Believe

For years, BIPOC has had to deal with neglect in their community from state and city officials. However, the issue of environmental racism is rooted deeper than the lack of policy and dodging of environmental laws. The underlying worldview of The United States which favors white supremacy and maximizing profits over the value of life is due to the foundation the country has been built upon.

The only way to create true change is through education and awareness. To create true sustainability for the planet and its people, our environmentalism must be intersectional. We must strive in acknowledging social and racial injustices, learning (and unlearning) from BIPOC people, and giving them a platform, breaking the cycle that has been dormant for decades.
